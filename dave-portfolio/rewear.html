Project Overview

Rewear is a marketplace for buying, selling or exchanging second hand items as well as purchasing new items or accessories. Connect with other community members and browse the thousands of items up for sale

Introduction

The goal of this project was to create an app. As a team we agreed that we would come up with a retail app that focused on sustainability, allowing the consumer to find vintage, new or even items owned by community members. I facilitated the UI/UX creating the hi fideility wireframes and the prototyping. Focusing a great amount of time on user focused design.

Exploring the problem

The detrimental effect of clothing waste on the enviroment is nothing short of surprising. Every day, millions of clothes end up in the landfill having been only been worn on average of 7-10 times. Each year, there are over 100 billion garments produced, 92 million tonnes end up in the landfill to be disposed of.

"92 Million Tonnes end up in landfills"

"Fashion Industry responsible for 20% of Global Water Waste"

"11.3 million tons of textile waste in America alone"

Preliminary Research

The information that was gathered during research

Target User

  • - Users who dont like fast fashion
  • - Users with limited additional income
  • - Users with extra unworn clothes

Potential Strategies

  • - Rewards: A reward for posting items up for sale.
  • - In app points: Users would be able to purchase items with points they earn.
  • - Membership: A cheap membership that would give users a certain amount of points every month, which can be used to purchase items on the app

Problem Statement

Most people have plenty of old clothes lying around in there closets. Unfortunately, they think about simply throwing it in the trash, not understanding how big of an impact it has on our enviroment. Users would be more incentivized to reconsider the way they get rid of there old clothes with a monetary return

"It takes 2,700 litres of water to make one cotton t-shirt"

Design Process

User Flow

To visualize the number of steps a user takes to complete an expected task on the app, identifying how the average user navigates through the app. The user flow will give an understanding of how to map out the screens as well as what the most optimal flow is.

Flow 1: Create an account or Login to an existing account

Wireframes

Done by Designer: Shay

Hi-fidelity Wireframes

Once the wireframes were done by my designer, we got together to work on the hi fidelity wireframes and the prototyping

Once the initial hi fidelity wireframes were finished, the team collaborated to make sure that it was up to standard.

The prototypes and the hi fidelity wireframes were done with making the process as simple as possible. We wanted to make sure that the user would know what they were looking at and what would come next, with minimal distractions. Focusing on the customer being able to complete there transaction.

Prior to creating the wireframes, research was done on competitive apps (Poshmark, Vinted, Grailed). Through research we found that they had one thing that they all offered, the ability to quickly upload items. This was down by adding it to the bottom nav bar. Therefore, we incorporated into our design which would allow the user to upload items with ease.

Takeaways

User Testing

Since this project strictly focused on completing a project using the waterfall methodology with the use of a project management tool, the next step would be to test the prototype on users. Is it easy enough for users to use? Are there more features that they want to see? Any changes that they would like to see? Those would all be questions that would further the process of the app

Building out a messaging platform

Users would be able to message each other in regards to trades or buying second hand items. It would cause us to think how the page would look like as well as the user experience i.e how can we make it so that users can message each other easily and have a good experience?
